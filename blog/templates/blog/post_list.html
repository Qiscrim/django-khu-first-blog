I was born and raised under the warm sun of Malaysia, where the air smells of rain and the sky is always full of colour.

Growing up, I was the kind of girl who asked too many questions, stayed up too late reading, and always had something on her mind.

Sometimes I look at myself and wonder — who is this person?

A student far from home, sitting in a classroom in Korea, learning things she never imagined she would learn.

There are days I feel lost, like a stranger in my own story. I turn away from the mirror and tell myself to keep going.

But when I look back, I see someone who crossed oceans, who chose the harder path, who kept showing up anyway.

And slowly, I stop hating that girl in the mirror.

Because she is still here — still trying, still learning, still becoming someone worth knowing.

Under the warm Malaysian sun, and now under Korean skies, Siti Nur Irdina is finding her way.
